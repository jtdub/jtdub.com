TAS Archaeological Field School 2025

Archaeological field school focusing on terrestrial site documentation and excavation techniques.

Program Overview

The Texas Archaeological Society (TAS) Field School 2025 represents an intensive training program in archaeological methods. As an excavation and documentation specialist, this project combines hands-on fieldwork with rigorous academic training.

Research Activities

  • Site Mapping: Systematic archaeological site mapping and feature identification
  • Stratigraphic Recording: Detailed documentation of archaeological layers and contexts
  • Artifact Processing: Cleaning, cataloging, and initial analysis of recovered materials
  • Digital Documentation: Photogrammetric recording

Learning Outcomes

The field school provides comprehensive training in archaeological methods while contributing to ongoing research. Participants gain experience in excavation techniques, archaeological documentation, and laboratory analysis.

Expected Outcomes

The project aims to produce improved site inventory and identification of future excavation sites, contributing to better understanding of regional archaeological resources.
