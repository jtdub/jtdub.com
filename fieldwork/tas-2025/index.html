TAS Archaeological Field School 2025

Underwater archaeological field school focusing on submerged site documentation and artifact recovery techniques.

Program Overview

The Texas Archaeological Society (TAS) Field School 2025 represents an intensive training program in underwater archaeological methods. As a scientific diver and documentation specialist, this project combines hands-on fieldwork with rigorous academic training.

Research Activities

  • Submerged Survey: Systematic underwater site mapping and feature identification
  • Stratigraphic Recording: Detailed documentation of archaeological layers and contexts
  • Artifact Processing: Cleaning, cataloging, and initial analysis of recovered materials
  • Digital Documentation: 3D modeling and photogrammetric recording

Learning Outcomes

The field school provides comprehensive training in underwater archaeological methods while contributing to ongoing research. Participants gain experience in scientific diving, archaeological documentation, and laboratory analysis techniques.

Expected Outcomes

The project aims to produce peer-reviewed publications on underwater archaeological methods and contribute to improved site inventory and preservation protocols.
