Sibinacocha High-Altitude Lake Research

Multi-year research project examining paleolimnological records and contemporary ecosystem dynamics in Peru's highest navigable lake.

Project Overview

The Sibinacocha research project represents a collaborative effort to understand the environmental history and current ecological status of one of Peru's most significant high-altitude lakes. Located at 4,873 meters above sea level, Sibinacocha provides a unique window into Andean climate change and human-environment interactions.

Research Methods

  • Scientific Diving: Submerged surveys and sediment core collection at extreme altitude
  • Bathymetric Mapping: Comprehensive lake floor mapping using sonar technology
  • Sediment Analysis: Paleolimnological reconstruction from sediment cores
  • Community Engagement: Collaborative work with local communities

Key Findings

Research is ongoing, with preliminary results indicating significant environmental changes over the past several centuries. The project has established important baseline data for future climate monitoring efforts.

Project Details

Location: Sibinacocha, Peru

Altitude: 4,873m above sea level

Duration: Multi-year project

Collaborators: Local communities, research institutions
