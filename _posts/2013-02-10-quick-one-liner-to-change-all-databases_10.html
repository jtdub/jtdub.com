--- layout: post title: Quick one-liner to change all databases tables to InnoDB in MySQL. date: '2013-02-10T16:49:00.001-06:00' author: jtdub tags: - Bash Tips - System Administration - MySQL modified_time: '2019-04-11T13:29:42.632-05:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-2200496390325245811.post-7058080593223894498 blogger_orig_url: https://www.packetgeek.net/2013/02/quick-one-liner-to-change-all-databases_10.html --- I've been attempting to get a better understanding of the operations of MySQL. For me, the best way to do that is hands on. I had a database, with a lot of tables, that I wanted to change the engine type to InnoDB. Obviously, being a lazy sys admin, I didn't want to change them all by hand. So, I made a quick one-liner to do the job for me. 

for i in `mysql somedb -e 'show tables\G' | grep -v ^* | awk '{print $2}'`; do 
    mysql somedb -e "ALTER TABLE $i ENGINE=INNODB"; 
done
