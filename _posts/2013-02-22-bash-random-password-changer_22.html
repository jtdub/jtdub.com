--- layout: post title: Bash - Random Password Changer date: '2013-02-22T17:33:00.001-06:00' author: jtdub tags: - Bash Tips - Pranks modified_time: '2019-04-11T11:09:55.539-05:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-2200496390325245811.post-5988240699359919389 blogger_orig_url: https://www.packetgeek.net/2013/02/bash-random-password-changer_22.html --- Here's a script that will change a user's password at random internals with a randomly generated 30 character password. :) 
#!/bin/sh
while true;
do
    for i in `< /dev/urandom tr -dc A-Za-z0-9_ | head -c30`;
    do
        echo 'someuser:$i' | sudo chpasswd;
        time=`< /dev/urandom tr -dc 0-9 | head -c5`;   
        sleep $time;
    done
done
