--- layout: post title: Think that you have a compromised Red Hat based system? date: '2012-10-12T19:09:00.001-05:00' author: jtdub tags: - Linux - Security - RPM modified_time: '2019-04-11T11:09:53.117-05:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-2200496390325245811.post-8210516761062177368 blogger_orig_url: https://www.packetgeek.net/2012/10/think-that-you-have-compromised-red-hat_12.html --- Use RPM to search for modified binaries.

rpm -Va | grep ^..5

This one-liner will use the RPM database to compare md5sums of all installed files and will give you a report of all files that have been changed from the default install. Configuration files may not be a big deal, but binaries with md5sums that don't match is a dead give away of a compromised system.
